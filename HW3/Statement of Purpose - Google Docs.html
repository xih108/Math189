Statement of Purpose 
As a computer science major student, I always long for studying further in this field not 
only because of my long-term interest but also my desire to explore new knowledge. ​I still 
remember​ how ecstatic I was when I typed “writeln ('Hello, world.')” in Pascal, my first 
programming language, and saw the output of “Hello, world.” for the first time.​ ​Everything was 
so attractive to me and I became immersed in coding. That experience sparked my interest in 
computer science.​ Admitted to UCSD as undeclared, I was provided with more choices to 
explore fields that I was interested in so ​I chose CSE8A as my first CS course. Unexpectedly, I 
found it to be totally different from what I learned before. Everything was new to me just like it 
was my first time learning programming. I exhilarated every time when I got my program work 
which built up my confidence and strengthened my interest. As I take more advanced CS 
courses, I realized that rapid changes take place in computer science all the time and it seems to 
have the magic that attracts me to explore more on those fields. Learning something new and 
challenging drives me to keep learning the latest knowledge. Aside from working hard in every 
cs course,I took online courses such as Machine Learning on Coursera and do a lot of search in 
order to explore more about computer science on my own. 
 
Because of my passion in computer science, I desire a chance for further education in computer 
science. Under such circumstances, the CSE five-year BS/MS program is the best choice as I 
can have access to graduate level courses and more ​research opportunities​ before ​getting a 
Bachelor's degree​. Furthermore, as a CS major undergraduate student at UCSD, I have the 
chance of knowing many professors under the CSE department which is a good resource for 
me to find interesting research. Computer Science has so many fields that attract me, among 
which, I have a particular interest in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. I know that 
Professor Kamalika Chaudhuri conducts research in Machine Learning on topics such as 
learning under sampling bias and she has an interest in confidence-rated prediction which is 
related to statistics. That research appeals to me and drives me to take CSE 151, Introduction 
to AI: A Statistical Approach with her this quarter. I have a solid background of statistics, as I’ve 
taken several related statistics courses such as statistics method and I also have experience of 
using R for statistics. I also studied through online courses on my own to expand my knowledge 
in this field. ​Therefore, the ​five year BS/MS program is attractive to me as it provides me with 
more resources and opportunities in CSE field. 
 
 
 
 
 
I have an outstanding academic standing with a 3.84 overall GPA and a 3.89 CS major GPA. 
Besides declaring a major in Computer Science, I am also double majoring in Applied 
Mathematics because I think the combination of them helps me comprehend material more 
thoroughly. Mathematics provides theoretical background and advanced algorithms for 
computer science while computer science can be used to verify those theories and apply them 
to broader fields. For example, when I took CSE158 and CSE151 which are courses related to 
Artificial Intelligence, I surprisingly found that these courses both used applied linear algebra 
knowledge. I also found through a computer graphics course that linear algebra can be applied 
to the transformation of simple shapes in order to generate a more complex diagram. I am 
suited for this BS/MS program because I have a solid background in both mathematics and 
computer science. I long for a further study in my academic career so that I can apply what I 
learned theoretically into practice. 
 
 
I have been a CSE tutor for more than a year and I am truly grateful for this opportunity as I have 
grown both academically and personally from this experience. As a tutor, I​ learn how to explain 
concepts that I am familiar with but new to students in an easier way. Such explanation not only 
makes students better grasp the material but also deepens my comprehension of knowledge. 
Sometimes, students ask topics that are not related to the course material which I have no idea 
about. I take this as an opportunity for me to expand my knowledge. Also, some students come 
up with good ideas that spark me. I learn from both students’ mistakes and their good ideas, 
which drives me to study further. Although I am very tired after long hours of tutoring, my 
tiredness is always relieved after seeing those students are satisfied with my assistance. 
Experiences of being a tutor under CS Department and a teaching assistant under Mathematics 
Department inspire me to help students achieve their maximum potential. Therefore, I am 
determined to be a university professor in the future. My plan is to first pursue a Master’s degree 
in Computer Science, ​specifically in the field of Artificial Intelligence. If I am admitted to this BS/MS 
program, ​I plan to join a research team to get more experience and prepare for a Doctor’s 
degree to catch up with the most advanced theory and technology.
